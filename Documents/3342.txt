BARE RUINED QUIRES' THE BINDING PROBLEMS OF ART LIBRARIES.
     
DESCRIBES THE BASIC STRUCTURE OF THE CODEX BOOK (ORIGINALLY A GROUP OF LEAVES
FOLDED AND GATHERED INTO QUIRES WHICH WERE THEN STITCHED TOGETHER AND TO OTHER
QUIRES AS NECESSARY). DISCUSSES THE PROBLEMS OF LARGE FORMAT AND HEAVY VOLUMES
AND MAKES RECOMMENDATIONS CONCERNING STORAGE. NOTES INADEQUATE ADHESIVE
BINDINGS AND RECOMMENDS THE DOUBLE FAN TECHNIQUE. BRIEFLY EXAMINES THE NEED
FOR, USE, AND CONSTRUCTION OF PROTECTIVE CONTAINERS. DESCRIBES A METHOD FOR
SECURING LOOSELY TIPPED-IN PLATES, BUT DISCOURAGES IN-HOUSE REPAIRS.
