THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN CENTRAL AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT' THE LOCAL VIEWPOINT.
     
DISCUSSES CUTS IN THE BUDGETS FOR LIBRARIES IN SCOTLAND IN THE LIGHT OF THE
GOVERNMENT'S CUTS IN PUBLIC SPENDING. ARGUES THAT MORE EFFICIENT AND SLIMMER
SERVICES WILL BE NEEDED IN FUTURE WHICH ARE MANNED BY MORE HIGHLY SPECIALISED
AND ADAPTABLE LIBRARIANS WHOSE SERVICES ARE RECOGNISED BY ADEQUATE PAY LEVELS.
STRESSES THE NEED FOR STANDARDS FOR SERVICES AND MEANS OF MEASURING LEVELS OF
PERFORMANCE.
