PROFESSIONAL EDUCATION CHANGES COURSE AGAIN.
     
CONSIDERS THE DESIRABILITY OF CONTINUING EDUCATION FOR LIBRARIANS, AND
DESCRIBES THE DIFFERENT WAYS IN WHICH UK LIBRARY SCHOOLS ARE PARTICIPATING IN
THIS PROCESS. DISCUSSES THE PROVISION FOR, AND POTENTIAL OF' SHORT COURSES;
PART-TIME 1ST DEGREE AND POST-GRADUATE COURSES IN LIBRARIANSHIP; TAUGHT MA
COURSES IN LIBRARY AND INFORMATION STUDIES; AND RESEARCH DEGREES. ALSO
CONSIDERS THE POSSIBILITY OF LIBRARY SCHOOLS BECOMING INVOLVED, THROUGH BEC,
WITH SUB-PROFESSIONAL LEVEL COURSES. ANALYSES THE RESULTS OF 2 SURVEYS
DESIGNED TO JUDGE THE EFFECTIVENESS OF THE LIBRARY SCHOOL CONTRIBUTION TO
STAFF DEVELOPMENT IN RECENT YEARS.
