CATALOGUING IS TOO IMPORTANT TO BE LEFT TO THE CATALOGUERS'.P
     
APER PRESENTED AT THE 1980 LIBRARY ASSOCIATION OF AUSTRALIA CATALOGUERS'
SECTION NATIONAL CATALOGUING SEMINAR, TO WHICH THIS ISSUE IS DEVOTED.
DISCUSSES THE AIMS AND OBJECTIVES OF CATALOGUING; CONSIDERS THE INTRODUCTION
OF COMPUTERS IN CATALOGUING; CALLS FOR CATALOGUERS AND ADMINISTRATORS TO TALK
TOGETHER; AND DISCUSSES THE USER'S REQUIREMENTS OF A CATALOGUE. SUGGESTS THAT
FOR CATALOGUERS 'SURVIVAL IS FALSEHOOD DETECTED'-CATALOGUERS MUST REMEMBER
THAT THEY ARE IN THE BUSINESS OF PRODUCING BETTER LIBRARIES.
