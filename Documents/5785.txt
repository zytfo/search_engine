BRIEFLY DESCRIBES THE TEACHING OF LIBRARY USE TO PATRONS OF THE REFERENCE
SECTION OF THE DAUGHTERS OF THE AMERICAN REVOLUTION LIBRARY AND STUDENTS OF
THE SCHOOL OF CONTINUING EDUCATION OF GEORGETOWN UNIVERSITY. DISCUSSES THE
INFORMATION PYRAMID WITH FACT AS ITS BASE, OPINION AS THE MIDSECTION AND VALUE
JUDGMENT AS THE CAPSTONE. LIBRARIANS SHOULD AIM TO FILL THE MISSING LINK
BETWEEN BASIC LITERACY SKILLS AND CRITICAL THINKING.
