EVALUATING BIBLIOGRAPHIC EDUCATION' A REVIEW AND CRITIQUE.
     
CONTRIBUTION TO A THEMATIC ISSUE ON LIBRARY USE INSTRUCTION. DISCUSSES SOME
REASONS FOR EVALUATING BIBLIOGRAPHIC INSTRUCTION PROGRAMMES. DESCRIBES THE USE
OF THE TEST, THE SURVEY, AND ILLUMINATIVE EVALUATION AS METHODS OF EVALUATION
BY VARIOUS US AND EUROPEAN EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS. CONSIDERS THE STATISTICAL
AND OTHER PROBLEMS INVOLVED WITH THESE TECHNIQUES, AND ARGUES THAT NONE OF
THEM ARE COMPLETELY SATISFACTORY, AND THAT IT MAY BE SOME TIME BEFORE TESTING
PROCEDURES CATCH UP WITH USER INSTRUCTION PROGRAMMES.
