BARRIERS IN INFORMATION COMMUNICATION.
     
EXPLAINS THE SIGNIFICANCE OF COMMUNICATION IN INFORMATION TRANSFER; LISTS
BARRIERS TO INFORMATION COMMUNICATION (LANGUAGE, FORMAT, TERMINOLOGY,
ACCOMMODATION OF NEW CONCEPTS IN EXISTING CLASSIFICATION SCHEMES, MISLEADING
TITLES, 'TRASH' LITERATURE, REPETITION, INADEQUATE BIBLIOGRAPHICAL CONTROL,
COST, TIME LAG, AND LACK OF INFORMATION AWARENESS); AND SUGGESTS MEASURES FOR
OVERCOMING SUCH BARRIERS.
