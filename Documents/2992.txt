PRACTICAL AND ECONOMIC CONSIDERATIONS.
     
CONTRIBUTION TO A THEMATIC ISSUE ON PUBLIC LENDING RIGHT (PLR). DISCUSSES THE
JUSTICE OF COMPENSATING AN AUTHOR WHEN HIS BOOKS ARE USED; THE USE OF PLR TO
ENCOURAGE NATIVE AUTHORS; PLR ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA; INTERNATIONAL ASPECTS;
EFFECTS OF EXTENDING THE SCOPE OF PLR TO ENCOMPASS ALL LIBRARIES AND ALSO BOOK
USE WITHIN LIBRARIES; PROBLEMS OF MEASURING BOOK USE; THE ROLE OF THE
CLEARINGHOUSE; FUNDING; LEVELS OF PAYMENT; COST OF PLR SCHEMES; PROCEDURES FOR
INTRODUCING A PLR SCHEME; AND POSSIBLE CONSEQUENCES OF PLR CONCERNING AUTHORS.
