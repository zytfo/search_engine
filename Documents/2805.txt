READER EDUCATION IN THE EIGHTIES.
     
REVIEWS SOME FEATURES OF THE DEVELOPMENT OF READER EDUCATION IN THE UK AND
USA, AND DISCUSSES THEIR APPLICATION TO THE AUSTRALIAN SITUATION. CONSIDERS'
THE QUESTION OF WHO SHOULD BE RESPONSIBLE FOR READER EDUCATION' THE NEEDS AND
EXPECTATIONS OF THE RECIPIENTS; THE RATIONALE FOR READER EDUCATION; ITS
METHODS AND CONTENT; AND THE TIMING AND DURATION OF PROGRAMMES. OUTLINES SOME
POSSIBLE FUTURE TRENDS, WHICH INCLUDE THE SETTING OF MORE REALISTIC AND
RELEVANT OBJECTIVES, AND THE MORE EFFECTIVE USE OF AUDIO-VISUAL METHODS.
