CLASSIFICATION POLICIES IN DENMARK.

AIMS TO GIVE SOME IDEAS OF CLASSIFICATION AND INDEXING POLICIES IN DANISH
PUBLIC AND RESEARCH LIBRARIES AS WELL AS A STATE-OF-THE-ART REPORT OF PRESENT
LITERATURE SEARCHING. DISCUSSES THE INFLUENCE OF NEW TECHNOLOGY AND THE
POSSIBILITIES OF UTILISING DATA SUCH AS SUBJECT ENTRIES FROM MARC TAPES FOR
CATALOGUING, AND PREDICTS A DEVELOPMENT WITHIN THE NEXT 10 YEARS TOWARDS
ON-LINE INFORMATION SYSTEMS IN 1 OR BOTH OF THE DANISH LIBRARY SECTORS, EITHER
AS LARGE SHARED SYSTEMS OR, PERHAPS MORE LIKELY, AS SMALLER LIBRARY DATA BASES
IN NETWORKS.
