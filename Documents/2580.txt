ALBANIA-A JOURNEY INTO THE PAST AND FUTURE.
     
DESCRIBES LIBRARY CONDITIONS IN ALBANIA. THE COUNTRY HAS A POLICY OF
SELF-SUFFICIENCY, WHICH CAN MAKE IT SEEM ISOLATED POLITICALLY AND
ECONOMICALLY. THE PUBLIC LIBRARY STRUCTURE CONSISTS OF THE NATIONAL LIBRARY
AND 26 CENTRAL, DISTRICT, LIBRARIES. THE LATTER COORDINATE LOCAL LIBRARY
ACTIVITIES. THE NATIONAL LIBRARY IN TIRANA, WITH 800,000 VOLUMES, HAS, LIKE
OTHER PUBLIC LIBRARIES, MAINLY TECHNICAL, SCIENTIFIC, POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC
WORKS, AND RECEIVES 15 COPIES OF EACH BOOK PUBLISHED IN ALBANIA. IT RUNS A
2-YEAR COURSE IN LIBRARIANSHIP. DESCRIBES VISITS TO LIBRARIES IN TIRANA AND
FIER AND TO THE UNIVERSITY LIBRARY IN TIRANA, FOUNDED IN 1957, WITH 550,000
VOLUMES. CULTURAL ACTIVITIES ARE GIVEN HIGH PRIORITY, BUT TAKE PLACE OUTSIDE
LIBRARIES IN CULTURAL CENTRES.
