PUBLIC LIBRARIES IN LOCAL COOPERATIVE SYSTEMS.
     
DEALS WITH NETWORKING AND ITS IMPLICATIONS FOR MEMBER LIBRARIES OF A
COOPERATIVE SYSTEM IN NEW YORK STATE. AFTER DESCRIBING BRIEFLY LIBRARY
DEVELOPMENTS IN THE STATE, DISCUSSES THE HISTORY, ORGANISATION, AND FINANCING
OF THE NASSAU LIBRARY SYSTEM. THE SERVICE PROGRAMME OF THE SYSTEM IS OUTLINED,
AND THESE ACTIVITIES ARE THEN RELATED TO STATEWIDE, REGIONAL, BICOUNTY, AND
LOCAL ACTIVITIES WHICH HAVE AN IMPACT ON LOCAL LIBRARY SERVICE. THE PROBLEMS
OF NEW YORK SYSTEM DEVELOPMENT ARE MENTIONED, AND THE BENEFITS OF
PARTICIPATION IN A SYSTEM PROGRAMME ARE DISCUSSED.
