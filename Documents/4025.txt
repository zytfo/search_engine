THE DIARY OF THE HUMAN RACE: LIBRARIES IN A TROUBLED AGE.
     
PAPER PRESENTED AT LIBRARY HISTORY SEMINAR VI 'LIBRARIES & CULTURE', AUSTIN,
TEXAS, 19-22 MAR 80. LIBRARIES ARE AN INDEX TO THE CULTURE WITHIN WHICH THEY
EXIST-PROVIDING A REPOSITORY OF WHAT HAS BEEN DISCOVERED, IMAGINED OR HOPED,
AND SERVING AS ARENAS WITHIN WHICH PEOPLE CAN GRAPPLE WITH PRESENT TASKS AND
ATTEMPT TO COPE WITH THE FUTURE. PRESENTS A BRIEF HISTORICAL REVIEW OF THE US
LIBRARY'S ROLE IN CULTURAL LIFE, AND HIGHLIGHTS PRESENT AND LIKELY FUTURE
DIFFICULTIES IN MEETING THAT GOAL, AS LIBRARIES ARE FACED WITH INFLATIONARY
PRESSURES, THE PUBLIC'S INCREASING USE OF TELEVISION INSTEAD OF BOOKS, AND
HIGHER LABOUR COSTS.
