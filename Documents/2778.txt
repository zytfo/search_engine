THE EFFECTS OF METAINFORMATION COST CHANGE ON INFORMATION AND METAINFORMATION
PREFERENCE IN A DECISION TASK.
     
(PHD DISSERTATION-SYRACUSE UNIVERSITY.) HYPOTHESISES THAT COST CHANGES HAVE AN
EFFECT ON PREDECISION INFORMATION AND METAINFORMATION (INFORMATION ON
INFORMATION) PREFERENCES. REVIEWS THE TREATMENT OF COST EFFECTS ON INFORMATION
USE IN THE DECISION LITERATURE, AND TESTS THE HYPOTHESES THAT' THE ORDER OF
THE COST CONDITIONS AFFECTS THE PREFERENCES, THAT AN INTERACTION EFFECT EXISTS
BETWEEN COST AND SEQUENCE, AND THAT IF COST HAS AN EFFECT ON PREFERENCES, THEN
METAINFORMATION PREFERENCES WILL VARY INVERSELY WITH COST.
