LIBRARY DESCRIPTION' WHERE MUSES ARE PRESERVED-THE DUTCH LITERARY MUSEUM AND
ARCHIVES CENTRE.
     
THE NEDERLANDS LETTERKUNDIG MUSEUM EN DOCUMENTATIECENTRUM WAS FOUNDED IN THE
HAGUE IN 1953. THE MUSEUM WAS GREATLY EXPANDED IN 1954 BY A FORMER CITY
ARCHIVIST OF THE HAGUE, DR. W. MOLL, WHO DEPOSITED ON PERMANENT LOAN HIS
PRIVATE COLLECTION OF 360 BOXES OF MATERIAL RELATING TO ABOUT 1,500 DUTCH
AUTHORS. THE COLLECTION NOW CONTAINS MANUSCRIPTS, CORRECTED PROOFS,
PUBLISHERS' CONTRACTS, BOOK ILLUSTRATIONS, PORTRAITS AND GRAMOPHONE AND
TAPE-RECORDINGS RELATING TO DUTCH AUTHORS OF THE POST-1750 PERIOD. THE MUSEUM
IS FINANCED PARTLY BY THE DUTCH GOVERNMENT AND PARTLY BY THE CITY COUNCIL AND
HAS AN ANNUAL PURCHASE GRANT OF APPROXIMATELY *64,000. IN 1982 THE MUSEUM WILL
BE REHOUSED IN THE NEW ROYAL LIBRARY BUILDINGS AT PRESENT NEARING COMPLETION
IN THE HAGUE.
