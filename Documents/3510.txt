VIDEO DISC FOR THE STORAGE OF OFFICE DOCUMENTS AND ENGINEERING GRAPHICS.
     
A DESCRIPTION OF THE OPTICAL DATA DISC, A MEANS OF CONVERTING HUMAN-READABLE
DOCUMENTATION TO HIGH-DENSITY STORAGE ON PHOTO SENSITIVE MATERIALS, IS GIVEN,
WITH DETAILS OF ITS DIMENSIONS AND MANUFACTURERS. ATTRIBUTES OF THE OPTICAL
DATA DISC, INCLUDING DATA ENTRY, CODE FORMAT, REPLICATION AND PROJECTED COST
OF BLANK DISCS, ARE OUTLINED. A COMPARISON OF OPTICAL DATA DISCS AND
MICROFORMS IS MADE, WITH REFERENCE TO STORAGE CAPACITY, MATERIAL COST AND
RETRIEVAL TIME. THE FUTURE AVAILABILITY OF THE DISC IS ASSESSED.
