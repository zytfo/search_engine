SOCIAL HISTORY AND ARCHIVAL PRACTICE.
     
THE ELABORATION, IN THE LAST 2 DECADES, OF A 'NEW SOCIAL HISTORY', CONCERNED
WITH SOCIAL STRUCTURE AND THE ATTITUDES, ACTIVITIES, AND DAILY LIVES OF
ORDINARY PEOPLE, WILL EVENTUALLY AFFECT ALL PHASES OF ARCHIVAL WORK. DEFINES
THE CONCEPT OF SOCIAL HISTORY AND REVIEWS IN DETAIL THE WAYS IN WHICH IT
IMPINGES ON ARCHIVAL PRACTICE.
