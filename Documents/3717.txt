LEIRFJORD LIBRARY' A SMALL COMMUNITY GAMBLES HEAVILY ON ITS LIBRARY.
     
LEIRFJORD, NORWAY, HAS A SCATTERED POPULATION OF 2,200 AND SMALL FINANCIAL
RESOURCES. IN MAY 80 NEW LIBRARY PREMISES OPENED IN A FORMER COMMUNITY CENTRE.
ADULT AND CHILDREN'S DEPARTMENTS AND THE LIBRARIAN'S OFFICE OCCUPY 127 SQ.M.
ON THE GROUND FLOOR, WHILE THE 1ST FLOOR WILL HOUSE STUDY-ROOMS, STACKS AND
STAFF FACILITIES. THE LOCAL AUTHORITY OBTAINED STATE HELP TOWARDS ALTERATIONS
AND EQUIPMENT, BUT OPERATIONAL COSTS ARE HIGH; HOWEVER, THE LIBRARY IS NOW
FULLY ACTIVE AND ITS USE IS STEADILY INCREASING.
