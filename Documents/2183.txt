PARTY-IN-POWER AS A DETERMINANT OF STATE AID TO PUBLIC LIBRARIES.
     
THIS STUDY MEASURES CHANGES OVER NEARLY 30 YEARS IN THE AMOUNT OF AID PROVIDED
BY STATE GOVERNMENTS TO PUBLIC LIBRARIES AND THE RELATIONSHIP OF THESE CHANGES
TO THE PARTY IN POWER. AID IS EXAMINED AT 2 LEVELS' THE 1ST VIEWS THE PARTY IN
POWER AT THE INCEPTION OF STATE AID, THE 2ND NOTES THE PARTY WHEN A
SIGNIFICANT INCREASE IS MADE IN AID LEVELS. NEITHER CONDITION PRODUCES A
DEFINITIVE RELATIONSHIP TO THE PARTY IN POWER AS DEFINED BY THE STUDY, AND
PROGRESS IN AID SEEMS NEARLY EVENLY DIVIDED BETWEEN THE ASCENDANT PARTIES, OR
AT LEAST IN PROPORTION TO THE DOMINANT PARTY ACROSS THE USA AT THE TIME.
