CONTINUING EDUCATION IN LIBRARY SCIENCE IN THE UNITED STATES.
     
A SUMMARY TRANSLATION OF A PAPER GIVEN AT THE UNIVERSITY OF SAO PAULO IN 1979,
SURVEYING THE US SITUATION, WHERE THE NECESSITY FOR CONTINUING LIBRARY SCIENCE
EDUCATION IS GENERALLY AGREED, BUT SATISFACTORY IMPLEMENTATION OF PROGRAMMES
IS NOT YET FULLY ACHIEVED. THE ACCOUNT COVERS ACTIVITIES TO DATE BY THE
LIBRARY SCHOOLS, PROFESSIONAL ASSOCIATIONS, AND NATIONAL BODIES; THE FIELDS IN
WHICH TRAINING IS MOST ESSENTIAL; AND THE PROBLEMS OF SPONSORSHIP FOR COURSES,
SYSTEMS OF OFFICIAL RECOGNITION, AND LOCATION AND ORGANISATION.
