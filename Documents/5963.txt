G.-F. DE BURE'S BIBLIOGRAPHIE INSTRUCTIVE, WITH ANNOTATIONS BY J.-F. NEE DE LA
ROCHELLE.

DESCRIBES A RECENT IMPORTANT ACQUISITION OF THE BIBLIOTHEQUE NATIONALE AND
DISCUSSES ITS SIGNIFICANCE. THE BIBLIOGRAPHIE INSTRUCTIVE WAS 1ST PUBLISHED IN
1584 AS A HANDBOOK FOR COLLECTORS. A BRIEF ACCOUNT IS GIVEN OF THE AUTHOR AND
HIS WORK. NEE DE LA ROCHELLE, A LATE 18TH CENTURY SCHOLAR AND DILETTANTE, USED
HIS COPY AS AN EVERYDAY TOOL. THE VARIOUS TYPES OF NOTES ARE DESCRIBED, AND
THEIR IMPORTANCE CONSIDERED IN RELATION TO FORMAT, BIBLIOGRAPHICAL REFERENCES,
CLASSIFICATION PROCEDURES, POINTS OF LEARNING, AND THE HISTORY OF THE PRINTED
BOOK, AS EVIDENCE FOR THE STUDY OF BIBLIOGRAPHY ON THE EVE OF THE FRENCH
REVOLUTION.
