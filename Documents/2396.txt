AN INDEXING SYSTEM' SWITCHING LANGUAGE FOR INTEGRATING AUTOMATED INFORMATION
NETWORKS.
     
(PHD THESIS-CASE WESTERN RESERVE UNIVERSITY, OHIO.) AN ACCOUNT OF THE BASIC
CONCEPTS, PROBLEM AREAS, AND OPERATIONAL CHARACTERISTICS AND TECHNIQUES OF
SWITCHING LANGUAGES, CONCENTRATING PARTICULARLY ON THE DESIGN AND DEVELOPMENT
OF CLASSIFIED CONCEPT COORDINATE INDEXING AS PROVIDING THE NECESSARY SWITCHING
MECHANISM FOR INFORMATION TRANSFER IN A NETWORK. IT IS BASED ON A SYNTHESIS OF
PRE- AND POST-COORDINATE INDEXING AND ENABLES FREE COORDINATION OF DIVERSE
CONCEPTS, THROUGH KEYWORD INVERTED INDEXING BASED ON AN 2A PRIORI1 SET OF
CLASSES.
