TELECOMMUNICATIONS AND LIBRARIES' A PRIMER FOR LIBRARIANS AND INFORMATION
MANAGERS.
     
LOOKS AT THE EFFECTS OF CHANGES IN COMMUNICATIONS TECHNOLOGY ON LIBRARIES.
CHAPTERS INCLUDE' LIBRARIES AND THE TRANSFER OF INFORMATION BY F.W. LANCASTER
AND DONALD W. KING; BASICS OF TELECOMMUNICATIONS BY BRIGITTE L. KENNEY; CABLE
TV AND LIBRARIES BY LYNNE E. BRADLEY; COMMUNICATIONS SATELLITES BY RITA G.
LERNER; VIDEOTEXT' IMPLICATIONS AND APPLICATIONS FOR LIBRARIES BY KATHLEEN
CRINER; FACSIMILE AND LIBRARIES BY JOAN MAIER MCKEAN; THE POTENTIAL IMPACT OF
OPTICAL DISC TECHNOLOGY BY CHARLES M. GOLDSTEIN; THE FUTURE OF THE LIBRARY IN
THE AGE OF TELECOMMUNICATIONS BY F.W. LANCASTER; ROADBLOCKS TO FUTURE IDEAL
INFORMATION TRANSFER SYSTEMS BY DONALD W. KING; AND A BIBLIOGRAPHIC
INTRODUCTION TO TELECOMMUNICATIONS BY DAVID DORMAN. THE FOREWORD IS BY EFREM
SIGEL.
