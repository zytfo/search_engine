MACHINE AIDS' A SMALL USER'S REACTION.
     
PAPER PRESENTED AT THE ASLIB TECHNICAL TRANSLATION GROUP CONFERENCE, LONDON,
20 NOV 80. A TECHNICAL SURVEY OF THE KINDS OF EQUIPMENT THAT MAY IMPROVE BOTH
THE QUALITY AND QUANTITY OF A TRANSLATOR'S WORK IS FOLLOWED BY CONSIDERATION
OF THE NEW CONCEPTIONS AND ATTITUDES IMPOSED BY SUCH EQUIPMENT. DISCUSSES THE
IMPORTANCE OF MOTIVATION AND OTHER PSYCHOLOGICAL FACTORS, AND STRESSES THE
NEED FOR GREATER ATTENTION BY MANUFACTURERS TO HUMAN FACTORS. CONCLUDES WITH A
PLEA FOR IMPROVED TELECOMMUNICATION FACILITIES AND MEDIA COMPATIBILITY BETWEEN
SYSTEMS OF DIFFERENT MAKES.
