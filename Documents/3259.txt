THE INFORMATION TRANSFER PROCESS.
     
A DETAILED DISCUSSION, BASED ON RECENT LITERATURE, WITH SPECIAL REFERENCE TO
THE SITUATION IN DEVELOPING COUNTRIES. COVERS THE PROBLEMS IN GENERAL OF
OBTAINING RELEVANT AND SIGNIFICANT SCIENTIFIC DOCUMENTATION, CREATED BY THE
INFORMATION EXPLOSION; THE EXISTING CHANNELS FOR INFORMATION TRANSFER, IN
RELATION TO BOTH AVAILABLE TECHNIQUES AND COMMUNICATION PROCESSES; THE
OBSTACLES INHIBITING EFFICIENT INFORMATION TRANSFER, BOTH GENERAL AND
PARTICULAR, AND SOLUTIONS PROPOSED; THE DIFFICULTIES OF OBTAINING INFORMATION
OF QUALITY, IN SITUATIONS WHERE THE INFORMATION CONSUMERS ARE FREQUENTLY ALSO
PRODUCERS; AND FINALLY THE STRATEGIC IMPORTANCE OF LIBRARIES IN THE
INFORMATION TRANSFER PROCESS. CONCLUDES BY OUTLINING PROCEDURES FOR ORGANISING
PROGRAMMES TO DEVELOP NATIONAL SCIENTIFIC INFORMATION SYSTEMS IN DEVELOPING
COUNTRIES.
