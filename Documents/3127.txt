AN UNEASY RELATIONSHIP? AUDIO-VISUAL RESOURCES IN PUBLIC LIBRARIES.
     
DISCUSSES THE RELUCTANCE TO PROMOTE USE OF AUDIO-VISUAL RESOURCES. TRACES THE
DEVELOPMENT OF THE CONCEPT OF PUBLIC LIBRARY SERVICES, SHOWING HOW LIBRARIANS
HAVE COME TO CONSIDER BOOKS AS THE CHIEF RESOURCE. USERS, HOWEVER, PRIMARILY
NEED INFORMATION, AND HERE AUDIO-VISUAL RESOURCES ARE ESSENTIAL FOR EFFICIENT
SERVICES. THE KINDS OF RESOURCES AVAILABLE ARE SURVEYED BRIEFLY IN CONCLUSION,
WITH SOME INDICATION OF COSTS AND PLANNING PROCEDURES.
