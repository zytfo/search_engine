CULTURAL INSTITUTIONS AND AMERICAN MODERNIZATION.
     
PAPER PRESENTED AT LIBRARY HISTORY SEMINAR VI 'LIBRARIES & CULTURE', AUSTIN,
TEXAS, 19-22 MAR 80. A SIGNIFICANT HISTORIOGRAPHICAL SHIFT HAS TAKEN PLACE
AMONG CULTURAL HISTORIANS WITHIN THE LAST 2 DECADES, AND A MORE COMBATIVE AND
CRITICAL STANCE TOWARD US INSTITUTIONS IS APPARENT, WHICH SERVES AS A BRIDGE
FOR THE PUBLIC LIBRARY DEBATE TO ENTER THE LARGER ARENA. IDENTIFIES AND
ACCOUNTS FOR THE HISTORIOGRAPHICAL CHANGE, RAISES SOME QUESTIONS ABOUT ITS
CONCLUSIONS AND THEIR RELATIONSHIP TO MODERNISATION. ALSO REFLECTS UPON THE
CONNECTION BETWEEN HISTORY AND CONTEMPORARY POLICY DEBATES.
