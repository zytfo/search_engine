THE PHYSICAL ENVIRONMENT AND COMMUNICATION.
     
THE INTER-RELATIONSHIP OF ALL ELEMENTS DETECTABLE BY THE SENSES IS IMPORTANT
TO THE DESIGN OF SPACES FOR USE BY THE HANDICAPPED. OFFERS EXAMPLES EMBODYING
THIS PRINCIPLE. QUESTIONS THE ASSUMPTION OF DIFFICULTY IN DESIGNING FOR THE
DISABLED PARTICULARLY IN VIEW OF THE DEMANDS MADE BY THE PLACE IN SOCIETY OF
MOTOR VEHICLES. MENTIONS ALSO THE OUTWARD APPEARANCE OF BUILDINGS WHICH MAY BE
INVITING OR DISCOURAGING.
