A LIBRARY FOR THE VISUALLY HANDICAPPED IN QUEBEC.
     
AN ACCOUNT OF AMERICA'S LARGEST FRENCH LANGUAGE LIBRARY OF BRAILLE BOOKS,
SERVING 900 USERS, ATTACHED TO A MONTREAL INSTITUTE FOR THE VISUALLY
HANDICAPPED. DESCRIBES THE ORIGIN AND DEVELOPMENT OF THE LIBRARY FROM ITS
FOUNDATION IN 1861, AND GIVES DETAILS OF CURRENT HOLDINGS OF BOTH BRAILLE AND
TALKING BOOKS. THE FUNCTIONS OF THE STAFF AND VOLUNTARY HELPERS IN COLLECTION
MANAGEMENT ARE DESCRIBED, AND THE OPERATION OF THE POSTAL LOAN SYSTEM
OUTLINED. PROCEDURES FOR SELECTING MATERIAL ARE DISCUSSED, AND THE ADVANTAGES
OF TALKING BOOKS OVER BRAILLE DEBATED.
