PUBLIC LIBRARIES AND NETWORKING' A BIBLIOGRAPHIC ESSAY.
     
MOST OF THE LITERATURE ON THE PARTICIPATION OF PUBLIC LIBRARIES IN COOPERATIVE
ACTIVITIES DEALS WITH THEIR EXPANSION FROM LOCAL ENTITIES TO MULTITYPE
SYSTEMS. WITHIN THE FRAMEWORK OF THIS EVOLUTION, TOPICS OF SPECIAL INTEREST
HAVE BEEN' APPROPRIATE UNITS OF SERVICE; LEGAL AND POLITICAL CONSIDERATIONS;
STIMULUS OF LEGISLATION; ROLE OF STATE LIBRARY AGENCIES; FINANCIAL ASPECTS;
HUMAN FACTORS; AND AUTOMATION IN NETWORKING. THE LITERATURE REVIEWED WAS VAST
BUT WAS DEFICIENT IN COMPREHENSIVE SYNTHESES, IN RIGOROUS, LARGE-SCALE
EVALUATION OF NETWORKING, AND IN STUDIES FOCUSING SOLELY ON PUBLIC LIBRARIES.
