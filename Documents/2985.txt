THE MODERN LIBRARY SERIES AND AMERICAN CULTURAL LIFE.
     
THE MODERN LIBRARY WAS CONCEIVED BY ALBERT BONI, A 25-YEAR-OLD GREENWICH
VILLAGE BOOKSELLER AND PUBLISHER, AS A SERIES OF HARDBOUND REPRINTS OF
IMPORTANT WORKS OF LITERATURE AND THOUGHT. IT LASTED FROM 1917 TO 1970, AND
THE SMALL, IN EXPENSIVE VOLUMES WERE STOCKED AT VIRTUALLY EVERY BOOKSTORE AND
MAJOR DEPARTMENT STORE IN THE USA. TRACES THE HISTORY OF THE SERIES, AND
INVESTIGATES ITS ROLE IN REFLECTING AMERICAN CULTURAL LIFE.
