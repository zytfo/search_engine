DIGITAL OPTICAL RECORDING, PRINCIPLES AND POSSIBLE APPLICATIONS.
     
DIGITAL OPTICAL RECORDING IS A NEW TECHNOLOGY BASED ON THE CAPABILITIES OF A
DIODE LASER TO MELT HOLES IN A TELLURIUM LAYER. 25,000 PAGES CAN BE WRITTEN
ONTO A DOR DISC. A SET OF DOR DISCS CAN FORM THE BACKBONE OF AN ELECTRONIC
DOCUMENT STORAGE SYSTEM WHICH CAN BE APPLIED IN VARIOUS ARCHIVAL APPLICATIONS.
