PREPARING FOR A SURVEY OF ALL THE LIBRARY PROCESSES. ONE LESSON OF AMERICAN
LIBRARY TRAINING.
     
CONTRIBUTION TO A THEMATIC ISSUE ON LIBRARY TRAINING. SUMMARISES THE AUTHOR'S
EXPERIENCES AT THE SCHOOL OF LIBRARY SCIENCE, KENT STATE UNIVERSITY, OHIO (SHE
IS A HOLDER OF THE GOMBOCZ SCHOLARSHIP). TO CARRY OUT EFFECTIVE AND ECONOMIC
HIGH-LEVEL LIBRARY WORK, SPECIALISTS MUST BE PREPARED TO MANAGE GENERAL
LIBRARY PROCESSES AS WELL. US LIBRARY SCHOOLS PAY SPECIAL ATTENTION TO
TEACHING THE ANALYSIS, PLANNING AND ORGANISATION OF SUCH PROCESSES.
