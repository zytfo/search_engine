NATIONAL BIBLIOGRAPHIES OF THE FINNO-UGRIAN PEOPLES IN THE SOVIET UNION.
     
THERE ARE 4.5 MILLION UGRIAN FINNS IN THE USSR IN 6 DIFFERENT AUTONOMOUS
REPUBLICS, EACH WITH ITS OWN LANGUAGE, SOME USING ROMAN AND OTHERS CYRILLIC
ALPHABETS. PUBLISHING IS VERY LOCAL AND ONLY NEWSPAPERS IN THESE MINORITY
LANGUAGES ARE OCCASIONALLY AVAILABLE OUTSIDE THE AREA OF ORIGIN. BOOKS ARE
LISTED IN THE 2SOVIET NATIONAL BIBLIOGRAPHY1, BUT ONLY IN THE RUSSIAN
LANGUAGE. EACH AUTONOMOUS REPUBLIC ISSUES ITS OWN NATIONAL BIBLIOGRAPHY, THE
OLDEST ESTABLISHED BEING THE ESTONIAN WHICH BEGAN IN 1946 AND APPEARS
QUARTERLY. DESCRIBES THE FORMAT AND SCOPE OF THESE LOCAL BIBLIOGRAPHIES IN
SOME DETAIL.
