IS BELGIUM HEADING FOR INFORMATIONAL UNDERDEVELOPMENT?.
     
PREDICTS IMMINENT INDUSTRIAL COLLAPSE UNLESS THE COUNTRY REALISES THE
IMPORTANCE OF INFORMATION AND MAKES IT AVAILABLE. IN GENERAL, BELGIUM HAS
FAILED TO KEEP PACE WITH DEVELOPMENTS IN INFORMATION RESEARCH AND TECHNOLOGY,
AND LACKS EQUIPMENT, TRAINED PERSONNEL, NETWORK ORGANISATION, AND EVEN
ADEQUATE COLLECTION CATALOGUES. THE WORLD'S INFORMATION RICHES CAN BE ACCESSED
THROUGH COMPUTER TERMINALS, TELECOMMUNICATION NETWORKS, AND DATA BASES.
GERMANY'S ACHIEVEMENT IS CITED FOR COMPARISON. A BRIEF SKETCH IS GIVEN OF THE
INUMERABLE POTENTIAL FIELDS IN NEED OF ADEQUATE INFORMATION SERVICES, AND THE
BENEFITS WHICH WOULD ENSUE FOR THE ECONOMY AS A WHOLE.
