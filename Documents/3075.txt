GEORGE POMPIDOU AND PUBLIC READING.
     
AN ACCOUNT BY THE FRENCH DIRECTOR OF LIBRARIES FROM 1964 TO 1975, WRITTEN
SHORTLY BEFORE HIS DEATH. DESCRIBES HOW GOVERNMENT INTERVENTION, INSTIGATED
CHIEFLY BY POMPIDOU, REVERSED THE DECLINE OF INTEREST IN READING BY THE
IMPLEMENTATION OF A 10-YEAR PLAN OF PUBLIC LIBRARY EXPANSION. THE ARTICLE IS
FOLLOWED BY SHORT TRIBUTES FROM 2 OF THE AUTHOR'S COLLEAGUES.
