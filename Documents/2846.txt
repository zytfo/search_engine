THE AUSTRALIAN DEPOSITORY LIBRARY SYSTEM FOR GOVERNMENT PUBLICATIONS.
     
CONTRIBUTION TO A THEMATIC ISSUE ON DEPOSITORY LIBRARY PROGRAMMES FOR
GOVERNMENT PUBLICATIONS. AUSTRALIA HAS NO SUCH PROGRAMME, ALTHOUGH THE FEDERAL
GOVERNMENT (THROUGH THE AUSTRALIAN GOVERNMENT PUBLISHING SERVICE) DEPOSITS
COPIES OF ITS PUBLICATIONS WITH THE STATE LIBRARIES AND OPERATES A 'FREE ISSUE
SCHEME' TO 19 UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES. EXAMINES THE AUSTRALIAN EXPERIENCE, THE
VALUE OF A DEPOSITORY PROGRAMME, AND ALTERNATIVE STRATEGIES FOR PROVIDING
PUBLIC ACCESS TO GOVERNMENT INFORMATION.
