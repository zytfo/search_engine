LIBRARIES, BOOKS, AND THE NATURE OF AMERICA: THE CREATION OF THE SMITHSONIAN
INSTITUTION.
     
PAPER PRESENTED AT LIBRARY HISTORY SEMINAR VI 'LIBRARIES & CULTURE', AUSTIN,
TEXAS, 19-22 MAR 80. IN 1836, US CONGRESS ACCEPTED A BEQUEST OF OVER HALF A
MILLION DOLLARS FROM THE ESTATE OF JAMES SMITHSON, TO ESTABLISH IN WASHINGTON
AN INSTITUTION FOR 'THE INCREASE AND DIFFUSION OF KNOWLEDGE AMONG MEN'. THE
RESPONSIBILITY FOR THE DISPOSITION OF THE FUND LAY WITH THE GOVERNMENT, SO THE
USE MADE OF IT HAD TO BE SEEN AS A POLITICAL STATEMENT. IT RAISED THE PROBLEM
OF WHAT KINDS OF INTELLECTUAL PURSUITS AMERICA NEEDED,-AN ISSUE THAT INVOLVED
IMPLICIT NATIONAL SELF-DEFINITION. GIVES A DETAILED ACCOUNT OF THE DEBATE,
CULMINATING IN THE ESTABLISHMENT OF THE SMITHSONIAN INSTITUTION.
