DATELINE-WASHINGTON.
     
AN ACCOUNT OF A TOUR OF THE RENOVATION WORK IN PROGRESS AT THE FOLGER
SHAKESPEARE LIBRARY, ACCOMPANIED BY THE LIBRARY'S DIRECTOR, O.B. HARDISON.
OPENED IN 1932 AS A GIFT FROM THE INDUSTRIALIST HENRY CLAY FOLGER, THE LIBRARY
CONTAINS MATERIAL ON AND ORGANISES ACTIVITIES CONCERNING SHAKESPEAREAN AND
ENGLISH RENAISSANCE STUDIES. THE RENOVATIONS INCLUDE THE CONSTRUCTION OF A
RARE BOOK STORAGE AREA AND A NEW READING ROOM.
