HENRY THOMAS HARE 1860-1921. AN EDWARDIAN PUBLIC LIBRARY ARCHITECT AND HIS
WORK.

(M.A. THESIS). HENRY THOMAS HARE WAS A SUCCESSFUL COMPETITION ARCHITECT,
INITIALLY FOR MUNICIPAL BUILDINGS BUT LATER FOR PUBLIC LIBRARIES. AN ACCOUNT
IS GIVEN OF THE SOCIAL, PUBLIC LIBRARY AND ARCHITECTURAL HISTORY OF THE PERIOD
FOLLOWED BY AN OUTLINE OF HARE'S CAREER AS AN ARCHITECT WITH AN EXAMINATION OF
HIS VIEWS ON THE PLANNING AND DESIGN OF LIBRARY BUILDINGS. PARTICULAR
ATTENTION IS PAID TO HIS 'LARGE HALL OR ROOM' CONCEPT WHICH POINTED THE WAY TO
OPEN PLAN LIBRARIES.
