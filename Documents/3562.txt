TRAINING NON-PROFESSIONAL STAFF' A QUESTIONNAIRE APPROACH.
     
DESCRIBES A QUESTIONNAIRE TECHNIQUE RECENTLY USED BY GLASGOW UNIVERSITY
LIBRARY AS PART OF A SEMINAR IN READER SERVICES TRAINING FOR NON-PROFESSIONAL
STAFF. THE QUESTIONNAIRE AND THE DISCUSSIONS PROMPTED BY IT ALLOWED A
CONSIDERABLE AMOUNT OF GUIDANCE ABOUT THE PERFORMANCE OF READER SERVICES TO BE
CONVEYED IN A SHORT TIME, AND IN A MANNER WHICH THE TRAINEES SEEMED TO FIND
STIMULATING. BRIEFLY ANALYSES THE QUESTIONS INVOLVED AND THEIR POSSIBLE AND
ACCEPTABLE ANSWERS, AND INDICATES HOW THIS TECHNIQUE COULD BE ADAPTED TO SUIT
DIFFERENT CIRCUMSTANCES AND REQUIREMENTS FOR STAFF TRAINING.
