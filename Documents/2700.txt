FROM THE STATE LIBRARIAN.
     
DISCUSSES THE BUDGET CUTS INFLICTED ON THE WESTERN AUSTRALIA LIBRARY BOARD IN
THE 1980-81 FINANCIAL YEAR, PARTICULARLY THE 10.24% CUT IN THE BOOK FUND.
STRESSES THE IMPORTANCE OF LIBRARIES IN MODERN AUSTRALIAN SOCIETY AND REGRETS
THAT GREATER USE OF LIBRARIES AND SUCH SEVERE CUTS HAVE COINCIDED.
